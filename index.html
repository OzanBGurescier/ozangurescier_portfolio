Projects

2023 – 2024 Robotic Design Team

  • Autonomous lunar which has an objective to collect lunar regolith and deposit autonomously.
  • I worked on the deposition system and designed the box that holds the regolith.
  • I used Onshape and SolidWorks to design and do FEA tests on the box.
  • The main challenge was designing a lightweight box that could hold a large volume of material.
  • The system could withstand the load of the regolith and move up to deposit the material.

2024 – 2025 Robotic Design Team

  • I worked on integrating every sub-system, which are excavation, deposition, electrical and locomotion.
  • I lead the team that designed the chassis, gearbox cover, electrical box and every sub-system connection.
  • The main challenge was decreasing the size of the robot while trying a new conveyor belt deposition system.
  • The rover was able to excavate and deposit and mechanical systems were functional.

2025 – 2026 Robotic Design Team

  • Main goal was to make a lighter robot than 2024 – 2025 robot with full autonomy.
  • I worked on integrating every sub-system, which are excavation, deposition, electrical and locomotion.
  • I lead the team that designed the chassis, gearbox cover, electrical box and every sub-system connection.

Posture Checker

  • The goal of this project was to make a device that warns the user when they have a bad posture by using notifications.
  • A gyroscope sensor and Arduino were utilized to measure the angle of someone's posture.
  • As a wearable device, the biggest challenge was to make it comfortable to wear for extended periods without taking away from convenience.
  • Fusion 360 was used to design the device, and the casing was 3D printed. The cloth and padding were sewed and glued to complete the product.
  • The device functioned as intended, would send notifications after 20 seconds of bad posture, and had a cooldown to avoid alerting unnecessary.

FRC 2023 – 2024

  • The objective of this robot was to pick up cube and cone shaped objects from either the ground, or a counter, and place them to their respective slots during the competition.
  • This year, the team took on a new challenge of using a swerve drive system, where I designed the chassis and electrical connection surfaces using Fusion 360.

Airfoil Experiment

  • This experiment was conducted as a part of the extended essay in physics of the IB curriculum.
  • Aim of the experiment was to understand the effect of the width and the angle of attack of an inverted airfoil on generated downforce.
  • Selig S1223 airfoil was selected for its high lift coefficient from NACA airfoils. The wing was 3D printed in sections to allow for width variation.
  • A wind tunnel with a flow straightener to achieve a more laminar flow was constructed.
  • Experiment results were as expected, where a wider airfoil resulted in more downforce, and the optimal angle of attack was approximately 15 degrees for maximum downforce.
